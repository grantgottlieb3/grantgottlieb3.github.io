Parallel Hybrid Electric Vehicle — Drive-Cycle Analysis

Built a MATLAB/Simulink model to study power split, SOC behavior, and fuel economy on UDDS (city), HWFET (highway), and US06 (aggressive) cycles. :contentReference[oaicite:8]{index=8}

Read Report (PDF) See Plots

Model Overview

The model integrates an ICE and electric machine with CVT ratio logic and a supervisory function block that allocates engine/electric power and maintains SOC near 0.5. The controller enforces minimum engine speed and power limits and uses regenerative braking when possible. :contentReference[oaicite:9]{index=9}

Key Findings

US06 (Aggressive)

Lowest fuel economy (~26.9 mpg) driven by high drag and power demand; SOC may exceed target due to heavy regen events. :contentReference[oaicite:10]{index=10}

UDDS (City)

Best fuel economy (~31.8 mpg) thanks to low-speed electric assist and frequent regen; SOC ends near ~0.61. :contentReference[oaicite:11]{index=11}

HWFET (Highway)

~29.9 mpg; reduced regen opportunities and higher aero losses at speed. :contentReference[oaicite:12]{index=12}

Energy Balance

Supplied engine + ESS energy ≈ sum of acceleration, drag, and rolling losses (first-law check). :contentReference[oaicite:13]{index=13}
