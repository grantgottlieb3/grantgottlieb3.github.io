Edwin — Next Generation Smart Door

High-level, patent-pending concept: a hands-free entry experience that can authenticate a resident and actuate the door hardware while preserving normal, manual operation.

Overview Gallery

Overview

This project explores a next-generation residential entry system designed for convenience and safety. The prototype integrates passive user authentication with a discrete actuation mechanism to unlatch and re-secure the door, while keeping everyday manual use familiar and unchanged.

Due to its patent-pending nature, detailed mechanisms, component selections, and control logic are intentionally omitted here.

My Role & Collaboration

Mechanical Design

  • Fixture and packaging concepts for door-jamb integration.
  • Rapid prototypes and fit checks for mechanism clearances.
  • Assembly approach prioritizing serviceability.

Cross-Functional

  • Worked closely with electrical and software teammates.
  • Aligned physical constraints with sensing and control needs.
  • Iterated based on testing and user feedback.

Customer Constraints

  • Safety, reliability, and unobtrusive operation.
  • Preserve normal door use, minimal user burden.
  • Respect privacy and enable local processing.
