Hybrid Electric Vehicle — Projects Suite

Three connected studies exploring hybrid powertrains from the vehicle level down to the motor drive and battery pack.

Parallel HEV (PDF) PMAC Drive (PDF) Battery Performance (PDF)

1) Parallel HEV — Drive-Cycle Simulation

MATLAB/Simulink vehicle model with ICE + EM, simple supervisory control to maintain SOC near 0.5, and CVT logic. Evaluated UDDS (city), HWFET (highway), and US06 (aggressive) cycles for fuel economy, power split, and SOC behavior.

  • US06: lowest mpg due to high aerodynamic load and power demand; heavy regen spikes possible.
  • UDDS: best mpg—electric assist at low speeds and frequent regen raise efficiency.
  • HWFET: mid-range mpg; fewer regen events, higher drag at speed.

2) PMAC Motor Drive — Sine-Triangle PWM + 3rd Harmonic

Modeled a PMAC motor drive with DC source + LC filter, three-phase inverter, and dq-current control. Sine-triangle PWM with third-harmonic injection increases modulation headroom. Tested torque steps (0 → ±400 Nm) at 500 rpm.

  • dq current tracking and phase currents/voltages under torque steps.
  • DC link dynamics vs battery current under load and regen.
  • Near-instant torque response limited by inductor dynamics.

3) Battery Performance — Equivalent Circuit & Efficiency

First-order equivalent-circuit Li-ion model with OCV–SOC table and Coulomb counting. Compared round-trip efficiency under 0.2C vs 2C charge/discharge and estimated energy needs for a US06-like drive window.

  • 0.2C/0.2C: highest efficiency (low losses).
  • 2C/0.2C: moderate; losses rise with fast charge current.
  • 2C/2C: lowest; short, high-current cycling amplifies internal loss.
