Starship Delivery Robot — Wi-Fi RC Toy

Remote-controlled replica inspired by campus delivery robots. Custom chassis and linkages designed in PTC Creo, driven by Arduino-based control of DC and servo actuators. Focus on mechanism design, packaging, and reliable wireless control.

View Demo (add link) See Gallery Source Code (optional)

Overview

This personal project recreates the look and motion profile of campus delivery robots at tabletop scale. The goal was to design a robust, serviceable platform that’s easy to iterate on: modular drivetrain, accessible electronics, and clear mechanical load paths for the body shell and suspension.

My Role & Tools

Responsibilities

  • Concept, CAD, and packaging of chassis/body in PTC Creo.
  • Selection and integration of motors, servos, and drivetrain components.
  • Arduino firmware for RC control, steering/throttle mixing, and lighting.

Hardware

Arduino (e.g., Nano/Uno), motor driver (dual H-bridge), standard hobby servos, Li-ion/LiPo pack, 2.4 GHz or Wi-Fi link, custom linkages.

Fabrication

3D-printed brackets/mounts, laser-cut or printed body panels, off-the-shelf wheels, soldered harnesses, quick-disconnects.

System Design

Electronics & Control

  • Arduino reads RC receiver/Wi-Fi commands and outputs PWM to motor driver and servos.
  • Optional lighting (status LEDs) and buzzer; battery monitoring for safe cutoff.

Mechanical

  • PTC Creo CAD for chassis and body; 3D-printed mounts for electronics and steering linkages.
  • Serviceability: top shell removes with a few screws; wiring looms labeled and strain-relieved.

Results & Lessons

What Works Well

  • Reliable steering/throttle response with simple mixing logic.
  • Rigid chassis with easy access to battery and electronics.

Next Iterations

  • Upgrade to brushless ESC for higher efficiency and smoother control.
  • Integrate ESP32 for built-in Wi-Fi + telemetry dashboard.
  • Refine shell geometry and attachment to reduce rattle.
